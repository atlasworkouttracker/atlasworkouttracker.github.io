Privacy Policy

Effective date: 3 May 2026 · Last updated: 3 May 2026

Summary

Atlas Workout Tracker runs entirely inside your own Google account. It does not operate any servers, does not transmit your data to any third party, does not use analytics or tracking, and does not retain your data outside of Google's infrastructure. The only data it accesses is the Google Sheet you open it on.

Who we are

Atlas Workout Tracker (the “Add-on”) is a Google Workspace add-on developed and maintained by an individual developer. Contact: aetherlabs21@gmail.com.

What data the Add-on accesses

The Add-on requests the following Google OAuth scopes. Each is narrowly scoped — the broader Drive or Sheets scopes are NOT requested.

  • auth/spreadsheets.currentonly — read and write access to only the Google Sheet you have open while using the Add-on. The Add-on cannot access any other spreadsheet in your Drive.
  • auth/drive.file — per-file access limited to files you explicitly open with the Add-on. The Add-on uses this to identify the active spreadsheet; it cannot list or read any other Drive content.
  • auth/script.container.ui — permission to display the configuration dialog and side-panel cards inside the Sheets host page.
  • auth/script.locale — reads your account's locale (language and region) to format dates and times correctly.

How the Add-on uses that access

The Add-on reads your spreadsheet's structure (week templates, RT day rows, advanced-settings cells) and writes new rows to it when you create a new week or initialize a new tracker. It does not read or write any cell unrelated to its workout-tracking purpose.

Where data is stored

  • Your spreadsheet — the only place workout data lives. The Add-on creates rows there; you own and control them.
  • An undo snapshot — a copy of the previous week's pre-finalize state, stored in your account's Apps Script UserProperties (Google's per-user key-value store, scoped to you). Used only to power the “Undo last week creation” action. The snapshot is automatically obsoleted after 24 hours and overwritten the next time you create a week.

Data sharing

The Add-on does not share your data with any third party. There are no servers operated by the developer; no analytics services, ad networks, or external APIs are contacted by the Add-on at any point.

Limited Use disclosure (Google API Services)

Atlas Workout Tracker's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Specifically, data accessed via the listed OAuth scopes is used solely to provide the Add-on's user-facing workout-tracking features, is not used for advertising, is not transferred to others, and is not used or read by humans except for security or legal compliance purposes, or with your explicit consent.

Children

The Add-on is not directed at children under 13. Use requires a Google account, which has its own age requirements.

Your choices

  • Uninstall — remove the Add-on at any time from your Google Workspace add-ons settings. This revokes all OAuth grants and prevents the Add-on from accessing your sheets.
  • Delete the undo snapshot — the snapshot auto-expires after 24 hours. To purge sooner, uninstall the Add-on (uninstall removes all UserProperties for the Add-on).
  • Delete workout data — your workout data lives in your spreadsheet. Delete cells, rows, or the whole spreadsheet using normal Google Sheets controls.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the Add-on's behavior changes (additional features, new scopes, etc.). Updates will be posted here with a new “Last updated” date. Material changes that affect what data the Add-on accesses will also be reflected in the OAuth consent screen, which you will see again the next time you authorize the Add-on.
